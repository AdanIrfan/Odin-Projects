Biryani Recipe

Bombay Biryani

To make biryani, marinate chicken (or meat) with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, and spices like turmeric, chili powder, and garam masala. Cook basmati rice with whole spices (cloves, cardamom, bay leaf) until 70% done. In a large pot, layer the meat and partially cooked rice, adding fried onions, mint, coriander, and saffron-infused milk between layers. Cover tightly and cook on low heat (dum) for 20–30 minutes. Serve hot with raita or salad.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken or meat
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Whole spices (4 cloves, 2 cardamoms, 1 bay leaf)
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced and fried
  • Fresh mint leaves
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • A pinch of saffron soaked in warm milk
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooking oil or ghee

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken or meat with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Wash and soak the basmati rice for 20–30 minutes.
  3. Boil water with whole spices (cloves, cardamom, bay leaf) and cook the rice until it's 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  4. In a large pot, heat oil or ghee and cook the marinated meat until it's almost done.
  5. Layer the partially cooked rice over the meat.
  6. Add fried onions, chopped mint, coriander, and saffron milk between the layers.
  7. Cover the pot tightly with a lid or dough to seal it.
  8. Cook on low heat (dum) for 20–30 minutes.
  9. Gently mix before serving. Serve hot with raita or salad.
