Chole Recipe

Punjabi Style

Chole, also known as chana masala, is a hearty chickpea curry made with boiled chickpeas simmered in a spicy onion-tomato gravy. It’s seasoned with traditional Indian spices like cumin, coriander, garam masala, and amchur (dried mango powder) for a tangy flavor. Often enjoyed with bhature or rice, this dish is rich, flavorful, and perfect for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas (or 1 cup dried chickpeas, soaked and boiled)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Water as needed

Steps

  1. If using dried chickpeas, soak them overnight and pressure cook until soft. If using canned, drain and rinse well.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1–2 minutes.
  5. Stir in tomatoes, turmeric, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the mixture turns soft and oily.
  6. Add cooked chickpeas and mix well.
  7. Pour in a little water and let the curry simmer for 10–15 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  8. Sprinkle garam masala and amchur powder, mix and simmer for 2 more minutes.
  9. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or bhature.
